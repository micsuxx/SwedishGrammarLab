Om

The Swedish preposition om has several common meanings. It is often used to talk about a person, topic, or subject, to indicate that something will happen in a certain amount of time, or to describe something that is wrapped or placed around something else. It also appears in many fixed expressions and idiomatic phrases.

Use Meaning Example Translations
Around / Surrounding
Location Indicates something surrounding or wrapped around something else. Ta en halsduk om halsen! EN: Put a scarf around your neck!
DE: Leg einen Schal um den Hals!
FR: Mets une écharpe autour du cou !
IT: Mettiti una sciarpa attorno al collo!
ES: Ponte una bufanda alrededor del cuello.
Future Time
Time Indicates that something will happen after a period of time. De kommer om en vecka. EN: They are coming in a week's time.
DE: Sie kommen in einer Woche.
FR: Ils viendront dans une semaine.
IT: Verranno tra una settimana.
ES: Vendrán dentro de una semana.
About / Concerning
Subject Introduces the topic of a conversation. Vi har pratat om dig. EN: We have talked about you.
DE: Wir haben über dich gesprochen.
FR: Nous avons parlé de toi.
IT: Abbiamo parlato di te.
ES: Hemos hablado de ti.
Subject Introduces the topic of a text or publication. En bok om Sverige. EN: A book about Sweden.
DE: Ein Buch über Schweden.
FR: Un livre sur la Suède.
IT: Un libro sulla Svezia.
ES: Un libro sobre Suecia.
Subject Introduces the person or thing being discussed. Det är synd om honom. EN: I feel sorry for him.
DE: Er tut mir leid.
FR: J'ai pitié de lui.
IT: Mi dispiace per lui.
ES: Me da pena.

Body Parts and Physical Conditions

When describing a physical condition that affects a part of the body, Swedish sometimes uses om where English uses no preposition or a completely different construction.

Han är smutsig om händerna. EN: His hands are dirty.
DE: Seine Hände sind schmutzig.
FR: Il a les mains sales.
IT: Ha le mani sporche.
ES: Tiene las manos sucias.
Jag fryser om tårna. EN: My toes are cold.
DE: Mir sind die Zehen kalt.
FR: J'ai froid aux orteils.
IT: Ho freddo alle dita dei piedi.
ES: Tengo fríos los dedos de los pies.
Du är röd om kinderna. EN: Your cheeks are red.
DE: Deine Wangen sind rot.
FR: Tu as les joues rouges.
IT: Hai le guance rosse.
ES: Tienes las mejillas rojas.
