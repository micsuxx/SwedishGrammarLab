Konjunktion Function Example Translations
Kopulativa konjunktioner (Addition)
och The most common conjunction in Swedish. In stressed position it is usually pronounced as ock, while in unstressed position it is commonly pronounced as å. The conjunction is used to connect words, phrases, and main clauses. Jag studerar svenska och engelska. EN: I study Swedish and English.
DE: Ich lerne Schwedisch und Englisch.
FR: J'étudie le suédois et l'anglais.
IT: Studio svedese e inglese.
ES: Estudio sueco e inglés.
samt Has essentially the same meaning as och ("and"), but differs in usage. The most important difference is that samt normally cannot connect two independent main clauses. It is primarily used in formal, academic, legal, and administrative language, especially when och has already been used or when additional clarity is desired. Kursen innehåller grammatik samt litteratur. EN: The course includes grammar as well as literature.
DE: Der Kurs umfasst Grammatik sowie Literatur.
FR: Le cours comprend la grammaire ainsi que la littérature.
IT: Il corso comprende grammatica nonché letteratura.
ES: El curso incluye gramática así como literatura.
både ... och Emphasises that both elements are included and equally important. Unlike a simple och, this construction explicitly highlights the participation of all linked elements. Similar paired constructions include såväl ... som, dels ... dels, inte bara ... utan också, and ömsom ... ömsom. Hon talar både svenska och tyska. EN: She speaks both Swedish and German.
DE: Sie spricht sowohl Schwedisch als auch Deutsch.
FR: Elle parle à la fois le suédois et l'allemand.
IT: Parla sia svedese che tedesco.
ES: Habla tanto sueco como alemán.
plus Originally a mathematical term meaning "plus", but increasingly used as a conjunction in spoken and informal written Swedish. It expresses addition and often functions as a more colloquial alternative to och. Två kaffe plus en bulle, tack. EN: Two coffees plus a cinnamon bun, please.
DE: Zwei Kaffees plus eine Zimtschnecke, bitte.
FR: Deux cafés plus une brioche à la cannelle, s'il vous plaît.
IT: Due caffè più una brioche alla cannella, per favore.
ES: Dos cafés más un bollo de canela, por favor.
Disjunktiva konjunktioner (Alternatives and Choices)
eller Used to present alternatives or choices. The speaker or listener is expected to choose between two or more possibilities. Vill du ha kaffe eller te? EN: Would you like coffee or tea?
DE: Möchtest du Kaffee oder Tee?
FR: Voulez-vous du café ou du thé ?
IT: Vuoi un caffè o un tè?
ES: ¿Quieres café o té?
antingen ... eller Expresses an exclusive choice between alternatives ("either ... or"). This construction normally triggers verb-second word order (inversion) in the clause introduced by antingen. When used with the meaning "regardless of whether", similar to vare sig ... eller, it is instead followed by normal word order. Antingen stannar vi hemma eller går vi ut. EN: Either we stay at home or we go out.
DE: Entweder bleiben wir zu Hause oder wir gehen aus.
FR: Soit nous restons à la maison soit nous sortons.
IT: O restiamo a casa o usciamo.
ES: O nos quedamos en casa o salimos.
varken ... eller Expresses negation and means "neither ... nor". Because the construction itself is negative, an additional negation such as inte is normally unnecessary. The construction can also be extended to connect more than two elements. Varken Anna eller Erik kom till mötet. EN: Neither Anna nor Erik came to the meeting.
DE: Weder Anna noch Erik kamen zur Besprechung.
FR: Ni Anna ni Erik ne sont venus à la réunion.
IT: Né Anna né Erik sono venuti alla riunione.
ES: Ni Anna ni Erik vinieron a la reunión.
vare sig ... eller Traditionally means "whether/(n)either ... (n)or" and does not itself express negation. For this reason, it is often accompanied by a negative word such as inte, knappast, or aldrig. However, in modern Swedish it is increasingly common to use vare sig ... eller without an additional negation. Jag vill inte resa vare sig i dag eller tomorrow. EN: I do not want to travel either today or tomorrow.
DE: Ich möchte weder heute noch morgen reisen.
FR: Je ne veux voyager ni aujourd'hui ni demain.
IT: Non voglio viaggiare né oggi né domani.
ES: No quiero viajar ni hoy ni mañana.
Adversativa konjunktioner (Contrast and Opposition)
men Connects words, phrases, or clauses and introduces a contrast between them. The conjunction may be reinforced by the conjunctive adverbs ändå ("nevertheless") or visserligen ("admittedly"), which then trigger inversion of the verb in the following clause. Jag är trött, men jag arbetar vidare. EN: I am tired, but I keep working.
DE: Ich bin müde, aber ich arbeite weiter.
FR: Je suis fatigué, mais je continue à travailler.
IT: Sono stanco, ma continuo a lavorare.
ES: Estoy cansado, pero sigo trabajando.
utan Similar to men, but follows a negation such as inte, aldrig, sällan, or knappt. It introduces a second element that contradicts or replaces the first. Unlike men, utan normally requires the same subject in both clauses. Han dricker inte kaffe utan te. EN: He does not drink coffee but tea.
DE: Er trinkt nicht Kaffee sondern Tee.
FR: Il ne boit pas de café mais du thé.
IT: Non beve caffè bensì tè.
ES: No toma café sino té.
fast Often functions similarly to men by introducing a contrast. However, it is more characteristic of spoken Swedish and has a more limited range of use. Note that fast (more formally fastän) can also function as a subordinating conjunction meaning "although" or "even though". Vi bor här fast huset inte är vårt. EN: We live here although the house is not ours.
DE: Wir wohnen hier, obwohl das Haus nicht uns gehört.
FR: Nous habitons ici bien que la maison ne soit pas à nous.
IT: Viviamo qui anche se la casa non è nostra.
ES: Vivimos aquí aunque la casa no es nuestra.
Förklarande konjunktioner (Explanation and Cause)
för Always connects two independent main clauses. It introduces the reason or explanation for what was stated in the preceding clause and is equivalent to "because" or "for" in English. Jag gick hem, för jag var trött. EN: I went home, because I was tired.
DE: Ich ging nach Hause, denn ich war müde.
FR: Je suis rentré chez moi, car j'étais fatigué.
IT: Sono tornato a casa, poiché ero stanco.
ES: Me fui a casa, porque estaba cansado.
ty A formal and somewhat old-fashioned conjunction meaning "for", "because", or "since". It is used to explain or justify something stated in the preceding main clause. Today, ty is found mainly in literary texts, ceremonial language, legal documents, and older styles of writing. Vi måste skynda oss, ty tiden är knapp. EN: We must hurry, for time is short.
DE: Wir müssen uns beeilen, denn die Zeit ist knapp.
FR: Nous devons nous dépêcher, car le temps est limité.
IT: Dobbiamo sbrigarci, poiché il tempo è poco.
ES: Debemos darnos prisa, pues el tiempo es escaso.
Konklusiva konjunktioner (Result and Conclusion)
så Connects clauses and expresses a conclusion, consequence, or result. The second clause follows logically from the situation described in the first clause. It is the most common conclusive conjunction in spoken and written Swedish. Det regnade, så vi stannade hemma. EN: It was raining, so we stayed at home.
DE: Es regnete, also blieben wir zu Hause.
FR: Il pleuvait, donc nous sommes restés à la maison.
IT: Pioveva, quindi siamo rimasti a casa.
ES: Llovía, así que nos quedamos en casa.
