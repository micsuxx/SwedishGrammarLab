What is a Causal Subjunction?

Causal subjunctions (kausala subjunktioner) introduce subordinate clauses that express a reason, cause, explanation, means, or motivation.

They answer questions such as Why?, For what reason?, or By what means?.

Important

  • Causal subjunctions introduce subordinate clauses.
  • They explain why something happens or how something is achieved.
  • Different causal subjunctions vary in formality and usage.
  • Subordinate clauses follow subordinate-clause word order.
  • Sentence adverbials such as inte normally appear before the finite verb in subordinate clauses.
  • Do not confuse causal subjunctions with conjunctive adverbs such as därför, alltså, or följaktligen.
Subjunktion Function Example Translations
därför att Därför att is one of the most common causal subjunctions in Swedish and corresponds to English because.

It introduces the reason or explanation for an action, event, or situation.

Important: Do not confuse därför att with the conjunctive adverb därför, which means for that reason.
Reason
Jag gick hem tidigare därför att jag hade huvudvärk. 		Reason

ES: Me fui a casa antes porque tenía dolor de cabeza.

eftersom Eftersom is the most common neutral causal subjunction in modern Swedish.

It corresponds to English because or since and introduces the reason for an action, event, or situation.

Unlike därför att, which often presents new information, eftersom frequently introduces information that is already known or assumed.
Cause

IT: Dato che il treno era in ritardo, siamo arrivati tardi alla riunione.
ES: Como el tren llevaba retraso, llegamos tarde a la reunión.

då As a causal subjunction, då means since, as, or because.

This usage is more common in formal writing than in everyday speech.

Do not confuse causal då with temporal då, which means when.
Cause
Då han inte svarade på mitt brev, ringde jag honom. 		Cause
EN: Since he did not answer my letter, I called him.
DE: Da er auf meinen Brief nicht antwortete, rief ich ihn an.
FR: Puisque il n'a pas répondu à ma lettre, je l'ai appelé.
IT: Dato che non ha risposto alla mia lettera, l'ho chiamato.
ES: Como no respondió a mi carta, lo llamé.
Cause
Då vädret var dåligt ställde vi in utflykten. 		Cause

ES: Como hacía mal tiempo, cancelamos la excursión.
emedan Emedan is a formal and somewhat old-fashioned causal subjunction.

It corresponds roughly to English since, because, or as.

Today it is found mainly in legal, academic, literary, and formal written language.
Formal Cause
Emedan det regnade hela dagen stannade vi hemma. 		Formal Cause
EN: Since it rained all day, we stayed at home.
DE: Da es den ganzen Tag regnete, blieben wir zu Hause.
FR: Puisque il a plu toute la journée, nous sommes restés chez nous.
IT: Poiché ha piovuto tutto il giorno, siamo rimasti a casa.
ES: Puesto que llovió todo el día, nos quedamos en casa.
i det att I det att is a formal construction used to explain in what respect two things are related or similar.

It corresponds roughly to English in that or insofar as.

It is mainly found in academic, legal, and formal written language.
Formal Explanation
Städerna har vissa likheter i det att båda har universitet. 		Formal Explanation
EN: The cities are similar in that both have universities.
DE: Die Städte ähneln sich dadurch, dass beide Universitäten haben.
FR: Les villes se ressemblent en ce que toutes deux possèdent une université.
IT: Le città si somigliano nel fatto che entrambe hanno un'università.
ES: Las ciudades se parecen en que ambas tienen universidad.
