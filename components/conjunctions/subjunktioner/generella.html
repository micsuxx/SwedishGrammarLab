What is a General Subjunction?

General subjunctions (generella subjunktioner) introduce subordinate clauses without primarily expressing time, cause, condition, purpose, or comparison.

The two most important general subjunctions in Swedish are att and om.

Important

  • Subjunctions introduce subordinate clauses.
  • The subjunction normally occupies the first position of the subordinate clause.
  • Subjunctions cannot be moved within the clause.
  • Subordinate clauses follow subordinate-clause word order.
  • Sentence adverbials such as inte usually come before the finite verb in subordinate clauses.
Subjunktion Function Example Translations
att Among subordinating conjunctions, att is as important as och is among coordinating conjunctions.

The conjunction att is always pronounced /att/, unlike the infinitive marker att, which is often pronounced /å/.

As in English, the conjunction att can often be omitted. It is most commonly used to introduce clauses in reported speech.

Att also introduces subordinate clauses with a nominal function. Such clauses may function as a subject, direct object, or object of a preposition.
Reported Speech
Han sa (att) han arbetade hårt. 		Reported Speech
EN: He said (that) he worked hard.
DE: Er sagte, (dass) er hart arbeitete.
FR: Il a dit (que) il travaillait dur.
IT: Ha detto (che) lavorava duramente.
ES: Dijo (que) trabajaba duro.
Subject
Att andra fuskar gör det inte rätt att fuska själv. 		Subject
EN: That others cheat does not make it right to cheat yourself.
DE: Dass andere schummeln, macht es nicht richtig, selbst zu schummeln.
FR: Que d'autres trichent ne rend pas juste le fait de tricher soi-même.
IT: Che gli altri imbroglino non rende giusto imbrogliare a propria volta.
ES: Que otros hagan trampas no hace correcto hacer trampas uno mismo.
Direct Object
Vi såg att planet girade. 		Direct Object
EN: We saw that the aircraft was turning.
DE: Wir sahen, dass das Flugzeug abdrehte.
FR: Nous avons vu que l'avion virait.
IT: Abbiamo visto che l'aereo stava virando.
ES: Vimos que el avión giraba.
Object of a Preposition
Du kan lita på att allt ordnar sig. 		Object of a Preposition
EN: You can rely on the fact that everything will work out.
DE: Du kannst dich darauf verlassen, dass sich alles regelt.
FR: Tu peux compter sur le fait que tout s'arrangera.
IT: Puoi contare sul fatto che tutto si sistemerà.
ES: Puedes confiar en que todo se resolverá.
om Om corresponds to att, but is used to introduce indirect yes–no questions.

Unlike att, om cannot be omitted.

Note that om may also be used elliptically to answer real or assumed questions.

In addition, om functions as a conditional or concessive subjunction in other contexts.
Indirect Question
Vi undrade om han arbetade hårt. 		Indirect Question
EN: We wondered whether he worked hard.
DE: Wir fragten uns, ob er hart arbeitete.
FR: Nous nous demandions si il travaillait dur.
IT: Ci chiedevamo se lavorasse duramente.
ES: Nos preguntábamos si trabajaba duro.
Elliptical Answer
– Kan du laga köttbullar?
– Om jag kan laga köttbullar! Jajamänsan! 		Elliptical Answer
EN: Can I cook Swedish meatballs? Can I ever! Absolutely!
DE: Kann ich Köttbullar zubereiten? Ob ich das kann! Aber natürlich!
FR: Est-ce que je sais préparer des boulettes suédoises ? Si je sais le faire ! Bien sûr !
IT: So cucinare le polpette svedesi? Se le so cucinare! Eccome!
ES: ¿Sé cocinar albóndigas suecas? ¡Sí sé! ¡Claro que sí!
Conditional
Om jag hinner kommer jag. 		Conditional
EN: If I have time, I will come.
DE: Wenn ich Zeit habe, komme ich.
FR: Si j'ai le temps, je viendrai.
IT: Se ho tempo, verrò.
ES: Si tengo tiempo, vendré.
Concessive
Om det är dyrt köper jag det ändå. 		Concessive
EN: Even if it is expensive, I will buy it anyway.
DE: Auch wenn es teuer ist, kaufe ich es trotzdem.
FR: Même si c'est cher, je l'achèterai quand même.
IT: Anche se è costoso, lo comprerò comunque.
ES: Aunque sea caro, lo compraré de todos modos.
