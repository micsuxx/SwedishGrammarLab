What is a Temporal Subjunction?

Temporal subjunctions (temporala subjunktioner) introduce subordinate clauses that express time.

They show when something happens, how long something continues, whether one action happens before or after another, or whether two actions happen at the same time.

Important

  • Temporal subjunctions introduce subordinate clauses.
  • They normally occupy the first position of the subordinate clause.
  • They express time relationships such as when, while, before, until, and after.
  • Subordinate clauses follow subordinate-clause word order.
  • Sentence adverbials such as inte usually come before the finite verb in subordinate clauses.
Subjunktion Function Example Translations
när / då As temporal subjunctions, när and då both mean when.

In modern Swedish, när is more common in spoken language, while då is more typical of formal and written language.

The expression nu när often corresponds to English now that.

Note that då can also function as a causal subjunction, meaning since or because.
Specific Time
När/Då han hade parkerat bilen gick han fram till grinden. 		Specific Time
EN: When he had parked the car, he walked up to the gate.
DE: Als er das Auto geparkt hatte, ging er zum Tor.
FR: Quand il eut garé la voiture, il s'avança vers le portail.
IT: Quando ebbe parcheggiato la macchina, si avvicinò al cancello.
ES: Cuando hubo aparcado el coche, se acercó a la puerta.
Now That
Nu när vintern är här ser allting ganska trist ut. 		Now That
EN: Now that winter is here, everything looks rather gloomy.
DE: Jetzt, da der Winter da ist, sieht alles ziemlich trist aus.

IT: Ora che l'inverno è arrivato, tutto sembra piuttosto triste.
ES: Ahora que el invierno está aquí, todo parece bastante triste.
Repeated / Past Situation
När jag var liten spelade jag fotboll varje dag. 		Repeated / Past Situation

sedan As a temporal subjunction, sedan corresponds to both English after and since, depending on context.

It may introduce an event that follows another event, or a period that continues from a point in the past.
After

DE: Nachdem er das Auto geparkt hatte, ging er hinein.
FR: Après qu'il eut garé la voiture, il entra.
IT: Dopo che ebbe parcheggiato la macchina, entrò.
ES: Después de que hubo aparcado el coche, entró.
Since
Hon har varit deppig ända sedan han försvann. 		Since
EN: She has been depressed ever since he disappeared.
DE: Sie ist deprimiert, seitdem er verschwunden ist.
FR: Elle est déprimée depuis qu'il a disparu.
IT: È depressa da quando lui è scomparso.
ES: Está deprimida desde que él desapareció.
medan Medan corresponds to English while.

It is used to describe actions or situations that occur at the same time.

Similar expressions include under det att, under tiden som, så länge som, and just som.

innan / förrän English before is normally translated with innan.

When the main clause contains a negation, or after expressions such as knappt, Swedish uses förrän.

Inte förrän corresponds to English not until.
Before
Innan vi gick hem tackade vi värdinnan. 		Before
EN: Before we went home, we thanked the hostess.
DE: Bevor wir nach Hause gingen, dankten wir der Gastgeberin.
FR: Avant que nous rentrions chez nous, nous avons remercié l’hôtesse.
IT: Prima di andare a casa, ringraziammo la padrona di casa.
ES: Antes de irnos a casa, agradecimos a la anfitriona.
Not Until

EN: You cannot run until you have learned to walk.
DE: Du kannst nicht laufen, bevor du gelernt hast zu gehen.
FR: Tu ne peux pas courir avant d’avoir appris à marcher.
IT: Non puoi correre finché non hai imparato a camminare.
ES: No puedes correr hasta que hayas aprendido a caminar.
Knappt ... Förrän
Jag hade knappt börjat måla huset förrän regnet öste ner. 		Knappt ... Förrän
EN: I had hardly started painting the house when the rain came pouring down.
DE: Ich hatte kaum angefangen, das Haus zu streichen, als der Regen in Strömen fiel.
FR: J’avais à peine commencé à peindre la maison que la pluie s’est mise à tomber à verse.

ES: Apenas había empezado a pintar la casa cuando empezó a llover a cántaros.
tills Tills is a shortened form of till dess att and has the same function.

It indicates that an action or situation continues up to a certain point in time.

English not until is expressed with inte förrän, not with tills.
Until
Vänta tills vi kommer! 		Until
EN: Wait until we arrive!
DE: Warte, bis wir kommen!
FR: Attends jusqu’à ce que nous arrivions !
IT: Aspetta finché arriviamo!
ES: Espera hasta que lleguemos.
Continues Up To a Point
Jag stannade kvar tills mötet var slut. 		Continues Up To a Point
EN: I stayed until the meeting was over.
DE: Ich blieb, bis die Besprechung vorbei war.
FR: Je suis resté jusqu’à ce que la réunion soit terminée.
IT: Sono rimasto finché la riunione non era finita.
ES: Me quedé hasta que terminó la reunión.
efter det att When English after introduces a clause with a finite verb, Swedish normally uses efter det att.

Either det or att may be omitted, but not both.

When followed by an infinitive construction, Swedish uses efter att.

IT: Dopo che avevamo fatto la spesa, abbiamo pranzato.
