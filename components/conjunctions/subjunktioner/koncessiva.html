What is a Concessive Subjunction?

Concessive subjunctions (koncessiva subjunktioner) introduce subordinate clauses that express contrast, concession, or an unexpected result.

They indicate that something happens despite a circumstance that might normally prevent it.

Important

  • Concessive subjunctions introduce subordinate clauses.
  • They often correspond to English although, even though, despite, or even if.
  • The subordinate clause presents a circumstance that does not change the outcome expressed in the main clause.
  • Subordinate clauses follow subordinate-clause word order.
  • Some concessive subjunctions are common in everyday speech, while others belong mainly to formal written language.
Subjunktion Function Example Translations
fast Fast is a common colloquial concessive subjunction.

It corresponds to English although, even though, or though.

It is especially common in spoken Swedish.
Contrast
Vi gick ut fast det regnade. 		Contrast
EN: We went outside although it was raining.
DE: Wir gingen hinaus, obwohl es regnete.
FR: Nous sommes sortis bien qu'il pleuve.
IT: Siamo usciti anche se pioveva.
ES: Salimos aunque estaba lloviendo.
fastän Fastän is a more formal variant of fast.

It corresponds to English although or even though.

It is more common in written language.
Concession
Hon fortsatte arbeta fastän hon var trött. 		Concession
EN: She continued working although she was tired.
DE: Sie arbeitete weiter, obwohl sie müde war.
FR: Elle a continué à travailler bien qu'elle soit fatiguée.
IT: Ha continuato a lavorare anche se era stanca.
ES: Continuó trabajando aunque estaba cansada.
trots att Trots att is one of the most common concessive subjunctions in modern Swedish.

It corresponds to English although, even though, or despite the fact that.

It is common in both spoken and written Swedish.
Strong Contrast
Vi spelade matchen trots att det snöade. 		Strong Contrast
EN: We played the match even though it was snowing.
DE: Wir spielten das Spiel, obwohl es schneite.
FR: Nous avons joué le match bien qu'il neige.
IT: Abbiamo giocato la partita nonostante nevicasse.
ES: Jugamos el partido aunque nevaba.
medan Although primarily a temporal subjunction, medan can also express contrast.

In this usage it corresponds to English whereas or while.
Contrast
Anna gillar kaffe medan Erik föredrar te. 		Contrast
EN: Anna likes coffee whereas Erik prefers tea.
DE: Anna mag Kaffee, während Erik Tee bevorzugt.
FR: Anna aime le café alors que Erik préfère le thé.
IT: Anna ama il caffè mentre Erik preferisce il tè.
ES: Anna prefiere el café mientras que Erik prefiere el té.
utan att Utan att introduces an infinitive phrase with concessive or contrastive meaning.

It often corresponds to English without followed by an -ing form.

It describes something that happens without another expected action or condition taking place.
Without
Han gick därifrån utan att säga adjö. 		Without
EN: He left without saying goodbye.
DE: Er ging weg, ohne sich zu verabschieden.
FR: Il est parti sans dire au revoir.
IT: Se ne andò senza salutare.
ES: Se fue sin despedirse.
om än Om än is a formal or stylistic concessive expression.

It acknowledges something, but indicates that it does not change the main statement.

It often corresponds to English although, albeit, or even if.
Concession
Det var ett intressant, om än svårt, problem. 		Concession
EN: It was an interesting, albeit difficult, problem.
DE: Es war ein interessantes, wenn auch schwieriges Problem.
FR: C'était un problème intéressant, quoique difficile.
IT: Era un problema interessante, seppur difficile.
ES: Era un problema interesante, aunque difícil.
hur ... än Hur ... än introduces a concessive subordinate clause with independent or unlimited contrast.

It corresponds to English however or no matter how.

The construction emphasizes that the result remains the same regardless of degree or manner.
No Matter How
Hur mycket han än försökte lyckades han inte. 		No Matter How
EN: No matter how much he tried, he did not succeed.
DE: So sehr er sich auch bemühte, er hatte keinen Erfolg.
FR: Peu importe combien il essayait, il n'a pas réussi.
IT: Per quanto ci provasse, non riuscì.
ES: Por mucho que lo intentara, no tuvo éxito.
även om Även om is a very common concessive subjunction.

It corresponds to English even if or even though.

It can express either a real concession or a hypothetical condition with inherent contrast.
Even If
Jag går ut även om det regnar. 		Even If
EN: I will go out even if it rains.
DE: Ich gehe hinaus, auch wenn es regnet.
FR: Je sortirai même s'il pleut.
IT: Uscirò anche se piove.
ES: Saldré aunque llueva.
om ... så Om ... så is a strong concessive construction.

It is often repeated or emphatic and corresponds roughly to English even if or no matter whether.

The construction emphasizes that the result will remain the same regardless of the condition.
Strong Concession
Om det så regnar hela dagen går jag ändå. 		Strong Concession
EN: Even if it rains all day, I will still go.
DE: Selbst wenn es den ganzen Tag regnet, gehe ich trotzdem.
FR: Même s'il pleut toute la journée, j'irai quand même.
IT: Anche se piove tutto il giorno, ci andrò comunque.
ES: Aunque llueva todo el día, iré de todos modos.
