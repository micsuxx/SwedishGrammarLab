What is a Comparative Subjunction?

Comparative subjunctions (komparativa subjunktioner) introduce subordinate clauses that express similarity, difference, manner, degree, or proportional comparison.

They are used to compare people, objects, actions, qualities, or situations.

Important

  • Comparative subjunctions express comparison.
  • Some express similarity, others express difference.
  • Comparisons may involve quality, quantity, degree, or manner.
  • Several comparative expressions are fixed constructions.
  • Comparative clauses follow subordinate-clause word order when they function as subordinate clauses.

Proportional Comparison
ju ... desto Ju ... desto expresses proportional comparison.

It corresponds to English the more ... the more, the less ... the less.

The second clause expresses a result that changes proportionally with the first.
Proportion

ES: Cuanto más aprendo, más divertido se vuelve.
